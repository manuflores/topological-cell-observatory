    A stem-like fate maintenance circuit drives a homology class during the embryonic development of C. elegans

    C. elegans, a model organism of developmental biology

    The worm Caenorhabditis elegans is a fantastic model organism for developmental biology for many reasons. It is transparent, and hence it is amenable to study using the microscope. This property allowed Sulston and Horvitz to completely map its embryogenesis cell-by-cell. Later on, they expanded this study to look at the division pattern post-embryogenesis. The remarkable thing from the cell lineage of C. elegans is that it is invariant: all individuals from this species will have an identical division pattern.
